Case study

Revenue operations workcenter redesign

A revenue systems rebuild focused on lead flow, handoffs, team visibility, coaching, and the operating rhythm behind sales execution.

+40%revenue lead efficiency through smarter routing and logistics
+20%team efficiency from a redesigned workcenter model
AI-enabledcoaching and comparative insights embedded into workflows

Problem

Lead handling depended on fragmented calendars, inconsistent handoffs, and limited visibility into where process friction was costing revenue motion.

Action

I standardized calendars, introduced overbooking logic, improved routing and handoff rules, and redesigned the team workcenter around data-backed prioritization.

Tools

Salesforce CRM analytics Behavioral data AI coaching Operational dashboards

Business impact

The system made lead ownership clearer, improved response capacity, gave leadership better comparative insight, and turned RevOps from cleanup work into a measurable growth lever.

Sales funnel attribution model

A custom multi-touch attribution model that connected acquisition, CRM activity, and transaction data so marketing decisions could be tied to full-funnel outcomes.

15+models generated to estimate and predict conversion rate
10+targeting opportunities surfaced for leadership
WeeklySigma reporting cadence for marketing decisions

Problem

Channel performance was hard to evaluate because web analytics, Salesforce activity, and purchase outcomes lived in separate systems.

Action

I unified source, behavioral, demographic, CRM, and transaction fields into an analysis-ready table and used logistic regression to estimate conversion probabilities.

Tools

Google Analytics Salesforce Sigma SQL Logistic regression

Business impact

Leadership got a clearer view of which marketing and CRM paths were associated with higher conversion, creating more concrete targets for budget allocation and CPA improvement.
